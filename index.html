About Haarlem

The City

Haarlem, the capital of North-Holland, is a town full of lively restaurants, culture and sights worth seeing. Often considered one of Europe's "hidden gems", the city will capture your interest with its rich history, beatiful flowers and amazing archetecture.

Museums

The city is filled with museums, mostly focused around the subjects of art, history and science. The Teylers Museum for example, which is the oldest museum of the Netherlands. A number of notable other museums are listed below.

  • Frans Hals Museum
  • Archeologisch Museum Haarlem
  • Historisch Museum Haarlem
  • Het Dolhuys

Restaurants

Around the city's central culture point; the "Grote Markt" you will find a number of cozy, welcoming and reasonably priced restaurants serving a plethora of delicious food from different cultures. You will quickly find yourself a great pizzaria, but more than that, some oriental places or even traditional Dutch cuisine! Esspecially the Frietkamer (literally translated: french fry room) serves some of the best fries you can find.

Architecture

Haarlem features mainly medieval architecture, such as the world famous "grachten", the native tall and narrow houses or the large church "Grote Kerk" in the middle of the Grote Markt (very creative names). A must see is the city hall of Haarlem, which is renowned for featuring beatiful art from a number of different painters, mainly from the late 17th century.

For more information about Haarlem, visit the official tourism website.

Haarlem Center

Central Neighbourhood

The city centre of Haarlem is a very pleasant place to spend your time throughout both day and night. Surrounded by “grachten” and featuring the city’s main square; the “Grote Markt”.

The neighbourhood is Haarlem’s most active, due to it being filled with cultural attractions, restaurants & cafes, gardens & grachten and numerous shops, both from well-known international retailers as well as local boutiques and craftsmen. If you are visiting Haarlem, it is almost a given that you will visit the centre, with how much you can find here. There is something here for everyone.

Restaurants & Cafes

Whether it is to meet with friends & family or to take a break from a day of shopping in the centre, the neighbourhood offers a wide range of restaurants and cafes that feature delicious meals and drinks. Some restaurants that are people’s favourites include: Ome Pietje, Sumo and De Frietkamer. For cafes, be sure to check out Big Babette or Meneer Paprika.

Gardens & Grachten

Due to the medieval style of the city, the centre is densely filled with buildings and as such leaves little space for parks. However, the neighbourhood features some very enjoyable gardens, such as the Wijngaard Tuin. As often the case in the densely populated Dutch cities in the “Randstad”, these gardens are often behind building blocks or apartments, so keep an eye out for them. Furthermore, taking a trip around the grachten that enclose the centre is a great short trip. The city centre is often most scenic when viewed from a boat, looking up on the lively streets of Haarlem.

Shops

The centre is of great importance to the city due to the number of shops that are found here. You will not have a hard time finding well known international brands such as Adidas or H&M. Thankfully, there are also smaller boutiques such as flower shops or artsy decoration stores. Here you could for example find lovely souvenirs.

Good to know: most shops are open from Monday to Saturday from 9:00 to 17:00, however, you can maximise your time in Haarlem by utilising the special “koopavond”, where many stores are open on Thursdays from 19:00 until 21:00.

Sports

Taekwondo in Haarlem

In the Haarlem area you will find 3 taekwondo schools. You can choose to visit one of the "Hwa-Rang Dragon" schools or the "Mudoin" taekwondo school. Depending on which location best fits your needs, you should find a suitable place to learn and practice the martial art. Links to both schools can be found below.

Sporting Clubs in the Area

Haarlem features a number of sport clubs to sign up and participate in (in addition to the previously mentioned Taekwondo schools). Check out the table below for links and info about the different clubs:

  • Canoe: Haarlemse Kano Vereniging HKV - Conoe Club where squads row on the grachten and rivers around Haarlem.
  • Fishing: Hengelsportvereniging Haarlem - Sports fishing club in Haarlem with over 8000 members that has existed for more than 100 years.
  • Football: Olympia Haarlem - Olympia is a sports club in Haarlem that offers a wide range of sports, from football to badminton and darting.
  • Football: SV Alliance '22 - Alliance '22 is a football-centered club in Haarlem that includes both men's and women's teams.
  • Football: SV Geel-Wit '20 - A football club from Haarlem South-West, Geel-Wit has roughly 600 members and enjoys success with both junior and senior teams.
  • Gymnastics: Bato Haarlem - Founded in 1896, Bato specializes today in the development of female gymnastics talent but also offers recreational lessons.
  • Hockey: H.M.H.C. Saxenburg - Saxenburg is a mixed sex hockey club that fields both recreational and competitve hockey squads throughout all ages.
  • Taekwondo: Hwa-Rang Dragon - Martial arts school that teaches Taekwondo Brazilian Jiu Jitsu. They have two locations in Haarlem and are open since 1998.
  • Taekwondo: HTA Taekwondo Mudoin - Taekwondo school with branches in numerous Dutch cities. Opened in 1990 by Kuen Sabumnim Y.K. Kim
  • Ice Skating: Kras Sport - Krassport offers a plethora of lessons in many different sports, from ice skating and swimming to track and triathlons.

Hwa-Rang Dragon

Taekwondo is a martial art that originates from Korea and is best known for its fast, powerful and often head-high kicking techniques. Since 2000 Taekwondo is featured as part of the Olympic games. At Hwa-Rang Dragon, which exists since 1998, Taekwondo's 5 core rules are being implemented:

  1. In Nae (Perseverance)
  2. Yom Chi (Integrity)
  3. Ye Ui (Courtesy)
  4. Kukki (Self-Control)
  5. Baekyeol Boogul (Indomitable Spirit)

Check out Hwa-Rang Dragon in either Haarlem Schalkwijk or Heemstede
